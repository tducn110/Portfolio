**1. Origin** — m bắt đầu từ messy ideas, không phải expert ngay từ đầu.
**2. Proof** — m có project thật: Finance Tracker, Unsaid Words, PingBall, Game Prototype.
**3. Invitation** — m không chỉ show bản thân, m còn muốn giúp người khác show strength qua portfolio.

Dưới đây là prompt gọn để m bỏ thẳng vào **Figma Make**.

Create a modern personal portfolio website for Nguyen Tam Duc, a Computer Science and Engineering student from Da Nang, Viet Nam.

The website should be in English, professional but personal, with a clean black/white style and subtle modern accents. The tone should feel like an AI-native full-stack product builder who turns rough ideas into usable products.

Main message:
“I turn rough ideas into usable full-stack products.”

Portfolio story:
Tam Duc is not only learning how to code. He is learning how to build complete products from idea to UI, database, API, testing, deployment, and refactoring. He started with messy ideas, small projects, and trial and error, then gradually learned to build real products such as a finance tracker, an anonymous emotional support wall, an event landing page, and a game prototype.

He believes everyone has their own strengths, even when they do not know how to show them clearly. That is why he also wants to build portfolio websites that help people present their story, skills, and projects in a clear and memorable way.

Sections:

1. Hero

* Heading: “I turn rough ideas into usable full-stack products.”
* Subheading: “I’m Nguyen Tam Duc, a CSE student from Da Nang building full-stack web apps, portfolio websites, emotional digital experiences, and game-inspired prototypes.”
* Buttons: “View Projects”, “Work With Me”, “GitHub”
* Quick facts: GPA 3.7, Da Nang Viet Nam, TypeScript / React / Next.js / Supabase / PostgreSQL

2. Who I Am
   Tell a short story: I started with small real projects and learned that software is more than UI. A real product needs problem solving, user flow, database design, API structure, testing, deployment, and continuous improvement.

3. What I’m Building
   Create cards for:

* Full-stack web apps
* Portfolio websites
* Finance tools
* Emotional web experiences
* Event landing pages
* Game-inspired prototypes

4. Featured Projects
   Use problem-solving project cards. Each card must include: Problem, Solution, My Role, Stack.
   Projects:

* Finance Tracker V3: budget-first personal finance app. Role: Team Lead, Backend, Database, Architecture.
* Unsaid Words, Shared Hearts: anonymous note wall with comfort replies and supporter moderation. Role: Creator, Product Flow, Frontend, Supabase data layer.
* PingBall Landing Page: student event landing page for tournament information and registration. Role: Frontend developer and layout planner.
* First Game Prototype: early game development experiment with shaders, HLSL, C#, and interaction.

5. How I Solve Problems
   Show a simple process:
   Understand the problem → Shape the flow → Build the system → Refactor and improve → Use AI carefully.

6. Why Work With Me
   Message:
   “I do not only build pages. I help shape the story, structure the message, solve the real problem, and turn unclear ideas into something people can understand and trust.”
   Add points:

* Product thinking, not only coding
* Fast learning and adaptation
* Responsible from idea to delivery
* Can work across UI, backend, database, and deployment
* Uses AI carefully to research, refactor, and move faster

7. Portfolio Website Service
   Heading: “Your strengths deserve a website that shows them clearly.”
   Explain that Tam Duc builds portfolio websites for students, developers, creators, and small project owners who want to present their story, skills, and projects better.
   CTA: “Let’s turn your story into a portfolio people can remember.”

8. Contact
   Heading: “Let’s build something useful.”
   Buttons: GitHub, Email, Contact Me.
   Footer: “Built by Nguyen Tam Duc — CSE student, full-stack product builder, and AI-assisted developer from Da Nang.”

Design requirements:

* Clean, modern, responsive layout
* Desktop and mobile versions
* Strong typography
* Card-based project showcase
* Subtle animations or motion hints
* Sticky navbar
* Case-study style project cards
* Clear CTA sections
* Use whitespace well
* Avoid generic corporate look; make it personal, thoughtful, and product-focused

Và đây là **story ngắn hơn** để m dùng làm core message xuyên suốt web:

I did not start as someone who already knew how to build polished products.

I started with messy ideas, small projects, and a lot of trial and error. At first, I thought building websites was mostly about making the screen look good. But through real projects, I learned that a product needs much more than UI.

It needs a problem worth solving, a clear user flow, a reliable database, useful features, testing, deployment, and the responsibility to keep improving it.

That is the kind of developer I am becoming: someone who can take a rough idea, break it down, build the first version, and refactor it into something clearer.

I also believe everyone has their own strengths. Sometimes the problem is not that people lack ability — it is that their story is not shown clearly enough.

That is why I want to build portfolio websites too: to help people turn their skills, projects, and personal journey into something others can understand, trust, and remember.

Cái còn thiếu cuối cùng là **visual direction**. M nên bảo Figma Make làm theo vibe này:

```txt
Clean black/white portfolio
Modern student-founder/developer feeling
Not too flashy
Strong storytelling
Project cards like case studies
Subtle AI/product-builder vibe
A little emotional, not cold corporate
```

Nếu m muốn portfolio nhìn “đắt” hơn, đừng làm quá nhiều màu. Chỉ cần **đen, trắng, xám, một accent tím/xanh nhẹ**, typography tốt, project cards rõ là đủ.
